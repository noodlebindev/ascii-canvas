## Why this works

- Four angle clusters follow the classic content framework (Pain → Mechanism → Proof → Offer), ensuring the content plan builds trust progressively rather than jumping straight to the offer — readers need to feel the problem before they want the solution.
- Three content units per cluster (Hook + Post + Thread) give enough variety to cross-post without repetition, and the hook lines are written as actual openers rather than placeholder descriptions, making the map immediately usable.
- The distribution line names both platform and cadence, which is the one detail that determines whether a content plan gets executed or sits as a document.
